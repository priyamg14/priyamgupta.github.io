About Me

Machine Learning • Dynamical Systems • Control Theory

I am a PhD researcher in the Department of Aeronautics at Imperial College London, working at the intersection of machine learning, dynamical systems, and control theory. My research focuses on developing data-driven linear and bilinear models to enable efficient and accurate prediction and control of non-linear chaotic dynamical systems.

My work combines approaches from statistical physics, including Mori-Zwanzig and Koopman formalism, with modern machine learning techniques to create interpretable reduced-order models. I integrate these models into model predictive control (MPC) and reinforcement learning (RL) frameworks for adaptive, real-time control applications.

Research Focus

Core areas of investigation

Reduced-Order Modeling

Developing novel Mori-Zwanzig latent-space Koopman closures for non-linear autoencoders, enabling interpretable reduced-order modeling of chaotic systems.

Adaptive Control

Creating adaptive model predictive control schemes that integrate interpretable ML with physics-based priors for real-time control of non-linear dynamics.

Reinforcement Learning

Implementing ensemble and probabilistic model-based RL for control of chaotic systems, improving policy stability via value-function regularization.

Physics-Informed ML

Designing physics-constrained neural networks that enforce physically meaningful properties in latent spaces for improved generalization.
